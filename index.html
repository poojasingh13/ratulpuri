Portfolio

Mr. Ratul Puri is the Chairman of the Board of Directors of Hindustan Powerprojects Private Limited (Hindustan Power). His vision and understanding of the Indian energy sector has enabled the organization to emerge as one of the leading integrated power players in the country.
During the last five years, under his guidance the company has partnered with best in class investors to be a part of its growth story. Today in a short period, the company has assets to the tune of USD $2 billion which is a standout achievement for any organization in the country.
His technology background with the understanding of energy sector has resulted in adoption of state-of-the-art technology in building the power plants. This aspect is evident in the commissioning of ~ 350 MW of solar PV assets (including India and internationally) and also its upcoming 2520 MW thermal power plant in the state of Madhya Pradesh.
The company growth trajectory is poised for the next leap with commissioning of 6,020 MW of power over the next few years in thermal, solar and hydro. Additionally, the company is also developing over 6,100 MW of capacity using conventional and non-conventional energy sources in India, Europe, and the USA.
He leads an organization that prides itself as ‘The energy people’ thereby nurturing them to write many glorious chapters in India’s story towards achieving energy sufficiency.
Ratul is an alumnus of Carnegie Mellon, the Pittsburgh-based global research university. He holds a bachelor's degree in computer engineering, mathematics, and computer science.

Indians keen on electricity, not so much on gold

Agenda before the new government: Serious challenges that we should overcome.
Not getting timely approvals or frequent change in the policies and guidelines are proving to be one of the big hurdles in front of the India power sector. In fact there are multitudes of approvals needed, sometimes from the same department more than once, for the same project . This results in huge losses and crucial management bandwidth being wasted. If the entire approval process could be streamlined into a time bound clearance mechanism, it would aid the industry significantly. Other challenges which the energy sector is facing is acute shortage of raw material which is coal or gas, high interest rates, challenges associated with land acquisition, health of SEBs which should be taken due care off. Changes in sovereign laws of countries, like Indonesia which has pushed up import prices of coal is also another challenge the sector is facing. There is a strong buzz that the government is planning to revoke the fuel supply assurances given to coal-based power projects that have failed to meet their commercial production deadline. However, there are various reasons which are beyond the control of the developers. This issue must be looked upon by the government before taking a final call. Read More..

Challenges Before Indian Energy Sector
Having experienced near double digit growth over the last decade, India now faces the challenging task of meeting the aspirations of young India. I believe, that the expectations – employment, better life – can only be met with solving the energy deficiency in the country. Thanks to numerous policy reforms over the past 20 years, the Indian energy sector has seen a shift towards addressing the needs of the consumer. However, the Indian energy sector today is facing a major dichotomy – one hand you have able and willing consumers who are ready to pay for the electricity and on other hand you a well equipped power generators willing to fulfil the demand. The inability of discoms to connect the consumers with the buyers has resulted in many thermal power plants lying idle. Adding to this, the long developmental cycle for conventional energy (7-10 years) and many other challenges has meant there will be only very limited incremental conventional power projects getting commissioned post 2017. All the while the energy requirement of Country will grow in significant proportion. Read More..

Hindustan Cleanenergy signs 30 MW and 20 MW PPAs for solar projects in Punjab and Uttar Pradesh respectively
With an installed capacity of ~ 350 MW generating carbon savings of approx 500000 tonnes per year, Hindustan Cleanenergy is the largest solar farm developer in the country The clean energy arm of Hindustan Powerprojects has been awarded three prestigious projects for developing solar farms in the states of Punjab and U.P. Hindustan Cleanenergy Limited, the largest developer of renewable energy in the country has executed its power purchase agreement (PPA) for two solar projects of 15MW each with the Punjab government and the third one 20MW solar project with the Uttar Pradesh government. The projects were won by the company through a competitive bid and are expected to go on stream during the current year itself. The company’s strong track record in commissioning solar farms with high level of energy generation indicates sound commercial viability of solar projects in the country.
